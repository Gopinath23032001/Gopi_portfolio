Brazilian E-Commerce Data Analysis

Python Pandas NumPy Matplotlib Seaborn Jupyter Notebook

Overview

This project delivers a comprehensive analysis of the Olist Brazilian E-Commerce dataset, covering more than 100,000 orders placed between 2016 and 2018. The work spans data cleaning, multi-dataset merging, exploratory analysis across customers, sellers, and products, and a rich suite of statistical visualisations — ultimately generating actionable business insights.

Dataset

The dataset comprises multiple CSV files from Olist Store, including customers, orders, order items, payments, reviews, products, sellers, and geolocation data across Brazilian zip codes.

Part 1 — Data Understanding

All CSV files were loaded into Pandas DataFrames and profiled for shape, column types, and summary statistics. Categorical and numerical columns were identified and documented before any transformation took place.

Part 2 — Data Cleaning

Missing values were quantified and handled across all tables. Duplicates were removed, incorrect data types were corrected, and date columns were converted to datetime format. Inconsistent category names were normalised, outliers in payment values were identified, and missing review comments were replaced with a standardised placeholder.

Part 3 — Merging

Orders were merged with customers, then with payments, and products were joined to order items. A master dataset combining orders, customers, payments, products, and sellers was constructed and used for all downstream analysis.

Part 4 — Business Analysis

  • Geography: São Paulo (SP) leads in both customer count and order volume
  • Payment: Credit card is the dominant payment method
  • Average order value: approximately 154.1 currency units
  • Top category: Cama_mesa_banho (Bed, Table & Bath)
  • Peak sales: November 2017, with Monday receiving the most orders weekly
  • Delivery: Average delivery time of approximately 12 days, with significant late-delivery incidents identified

Part 5 — Customer Analysis

The top 10 highest-spending customers were identified. São Paulo was confirmed as the leading state by revenue. 12,489 customers placed repeat orders, indicating a meaningful loyal base. The majority of customer reviews were 5-star ratings.

Part 6 — Seller Analysis

Top revenue-generating sellers were ranked. Fastest-delivering sellers and those receiving poor reviews were identified. Product category distribution per seller was examined and compared across Brazilian states.

Part 7 — Product Analysis

Most popular categories by volume and by revenue were ranked. Categories attracting poor reviews were flagged. Shipping costs were compared by category, with computers and furniture incurring the highest costs.

Part 8 — Time Series Analysis

Monthly sales trends from September 2016 to October 2018 were tracked, showing year-over-year growth and seasonal patterns. Monthly customer growth and revenue trends were plotted. Delivery delay patterns were examined over time.

Part 9 — Matplotlib Visualisations

  • Bar chart: top product categories
  • Line chart: monthly sales trends
  • Histogram: payment value distribution
  • Pie chart: payment methods breakdown
  • Scatter plot: shipping cost vs review score
  • Horizontal bar chart: top sellers by revenue
  • Boxplot: product price distribution
  • Subplot dashboard for consolidated business insights

Part 10 — Seaborn Visualisations

  • Countplot for payment methods
  • Barplot for top-selling categories
  • Histplot with KDE for order value distribution
  • Boxplot for delivery times
  • Heatmap for numerical feature correlations

Key Insights

  • São Paulo dominates both customer concentration and revenue — a key market for targeted campaigns
  • November 2017 peak sales suggest strong holiday-season demand, with year-round growth confirmed
  • 12,489 repeat customers represent a loyal base worth retention investment
  • Credit cards dominate payments; instalment offerings could drive higher average order values
  • Late deliveries are a significant operational issue, particularly outside São Paulo
  • Home goods lead in volume; electronics command the highest prices and shipping costs

How to Run

Install dependencies with pip install pandas numpy matplotlib seaborn kagglehub, download the dataset using kagglehub (automated in the notebook), then run all cells sequentially in Jupyter Notebook.
